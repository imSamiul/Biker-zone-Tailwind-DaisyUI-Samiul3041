What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Most manufacturers recommend replacing your helmet every 5-10 years, even if it hasn't been in an accident. Over time, the materials in the helmet can degrade, especially with exposure to sunlight and extreme temperatures. This can compromise the helmet's ability to protect your head in a crash.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

General Safety: Wear a Helmet: This is the most crucial rule. Always wear a properly fitted helmet that meets safety standards. Be Visible: Use bright clothing, especially during low-light conditions. Consider reflective gear to enhance visibility at night. Obey Traffic Laws: Treat your bike as a vehicle. Stop at red lights and stop signs, follow lane markings, and yield the right of way as appropriate. Ride Predictably: Signal your intentions clearly before turning or changing lanes. Maintain a steady course and avoid weaving in and out of traffic. Ride Defensively: Assume other drivers might not see you. Be aware of your surroundings and anticipate potential hazards. Ride at a Safe Speed: Adjust your speed based on road conditions, traffic, and weather. Avoid riding too fast, especially in unfamiliar areas.
